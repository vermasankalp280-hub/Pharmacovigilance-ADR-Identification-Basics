# 🩺 ADR Identification Basics – Pharmacovigilance Case Study

## Overview

This repository presents a sample case study on the identification and assessment of an Adverse Drug Reaction (ADR) using standard pharmacovigilance principles.

The project was developed as part of the **VirtualWorks Pharmacovigilance Virtual Internship** to demonstrate practical knowledge in ADR detection, causality assessment, and patient safety reporting.

---

## Project Objectives

- Understand the fundamentals of Pharmacovigilance.
- Identify potential Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs).
- Evaluate drug-event relationships using standard assessment methods.
- Apply WHO-UMC Causality Assessment.
- Apply the Naranjo Probability Scale.
- Understand the importance of ADR reporting in improving medication safety.

---

## Case Summary

A 45-year-old male patient receiving Amoxicillin therapy developed:

- Generalized skin rash
- Pruritus (itching)
- Mild swelling of the lips

Symptoms appeared three days after initiation of therapy and improved following discontinuation of Amoxicillin and administration of antihistamines.

---

## Suspected Drug

**Amoxicillin**

---

## ADR Classification

**Type B (Hypersensitivity Reaction)**

---

## Causality Assessment

### WHO-UMC Classification

**Probable/Likely**

### Naranjo Algorithm Score

**6 (Probable ADR)**

---

## Management

- Discontinue suspected medication
- Administer antihistamines
- Monitor patient condition
- Document penicillin allergy
- Report ADR to Pharmacovigilance Programme of India (PvPI)
